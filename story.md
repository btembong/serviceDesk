# UBFinance ServiceDesk — The Story

---

## The Story of the System

Imagine you are a customer of **UBFinance**, a bank. One morning you wake up and discover your debit card has been used for a transaction you never made. You are worried. You need help — fast.

In the old world, you would drive to a branch, join a long queue, explain your problem to a teller, get handed a paper form, fill it in, and then wait days for a callback that may never come. Your problem sits in a physical folder on someone's desk.

**UBFinance ServiceDesk changes that entirely.**

You open your phone, log in to the platform, select "Unauthorized Transaction", describe what happened, and submit. Within seconds your case is in a queue, flagged as urgent, assigned to an agent, and you receive a confirmation. You can track its progress from your phone. The agent resolves it and you are notified. The entire interaction is logged, traceable, and compliant with banking regulations.

That is what we are building.

---

## What It Is, Precisely

**UBFinance ServiceDesk is a digital support ticket management system built exclusively for a banking institution.**

It is not a chat app. It is not a simple contact form. It is a structured, secure, workflow-driven platform where every customer complaint, request, or enquiry becomes a **ticket** — a formal record with a lifecycle, an owner, a status, and an audit trail.

A ticket is born when a customer submits a request. It lives through stages — pending, under review, escalated, resolved, or rejected. It carries with it all the evidence needed to action it — the customer's identity documents, their selfie, their account details, their description of the problem. And it dies only when the matter is fully closed and logged.

---

## The Three Worlds Inside It

The platform exists simultaneously in three worlds, one for each type of user:

**The Customer's World** is one of simplicity and transparency. The customer sees only what concerns them — their own tickets, their own identity verification status, their own notifications. They are guided through processes step by step. When they need to prove who they are, the system walks them through uploading their ID and taking a live selfie. They do not need to understand the complexity behind it. They just need their problem solved.

**The Agent's World** is one of order and action. Agents are the human processors of the system. They receive queues of tickets sorted by priority and type. They review the evidence submitted — the ID scan, the selfie, the account details — and they make decisions. They approve. They reject. They escalate. They leave notes. They are the hands that move tickets forward. The platform gives them everything they need to make fast, informed decisions without ever leaving their screen.

**The Administrator's World** is one of oversight and intelligence. Admins see everything. They see every ticket across every agent. They see patterns — which ticket types are surging, which agents are performing, where bottlenecks form. They manage users, configure the system, and ensure the platform is operating within compliance. Their dashboard is not a workspace for individual cases — it is a control room for the entire operation.

## The Role of Identity in Everything

What makes this platform distinct from a generic helpdesk is the deep integration of **identity verification** — KYC, which stands for Know Your Customer.

In banking, you cannot simply trust that the person typing on a keyboard is who they claim to be. Before a PIN is reset, before an account is unlocked, before sensitive information is changed — the system must be confident of identity.

This is achieved through two technologies working in tandem. First, **OCR** — Optical Character Recognition — reads the customer's uploaded ID card and extracts their name, date of birth, and ID number from the physical document. Second, **facial recognition** compares the live selfie the customer takes with the photograph on that same ID card. If the face matches and the document is authentic, identity is confirmed. If not, the ticket is flagged for manual review.

This is not optional. It is woven into the very fabric of how certain tickets are processed. It is what makes the platform trustworthy enough to handle real banking actions.

## What Flows Through It

At its core, the platform is a **pipeline for human problems**.

A person has a problem. That problem is described, categorized, evidenced, submitted, assigned, reviewed, actioned, and resolved. Every step is recorded. Nothing is lost. Nothing is ambiguous. The customer knows where their case stands. The agent knows what to do next. The admin knows whether the system is healthy.

The pipeline handles eight categories of problems:

- Proving who you are so your account can be accessed
- Resetting your PIN or credentials securely
- Resolving failed or unauthorized transactions
- Managing your debit or credit card
- Enquiring about loans or credit
- Getting back into your mobile or internet banking
- Updating your personal information on file
- Raising a complaint or leaving feedback

These are not arbitrary categories. They represent the most common and most sensitive interactions a bank customer has with their institution. Each one has its own form, its own required evidence, its own processing rules.

---

## What It Produces Over Time

Beyond solving individual problems, the platform **accumulates intelligence**.

Every ticket closed is a data point. Over weeks and months, patterns emerge. Perhaps PIN resets spike on Monday mornings after the weekend. Perhaps unauthorized transaction reports cluster around a particular merchant. Perhaps one agent resolves fraud cases in half the time of their peers. The analytics dashboard surfaces these patterns so that leadership can act on them — retraining agents, adjusting staffing, identifying fraud vectors, improving self-service options to reduce ticket volume entirely.

There is also a complete **audit trail** — an immutable record of every action taken in the system. Who created a ticket. Who reviewed it. What decision was made. When. This is not optional for a bank. It is a legal and regulatory requirement. The platform makes compliance automatic rather than manual.
